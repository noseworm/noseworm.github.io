Michael Noseworthy

I am a graduate student in the Robust Robotics Group at MIT.

My research focuses on robust planning under uncertainty motivated by long-horizon manipulation tasks such as assembly or rearrangment. I am also a part-time intern at the NVIDIA Seattle Robotics Lab where I work on contact-rich manipulation. Before MIT, I studied dialogue systems at McGill University's Reasoning and Learning Lab.
